# Core assumptions

## Unit economics

| Assumption | Value | Source / basis |
|---|---|---|
| Tote cost (bulk) | $20 / tote | Reusable Transport Packaging benchmark; validate with quote |
| Tote lifespan | 250 rentals | Industry standard for industrial plastic totes |
| Tote amortization | $0.08 / rental | $20 / 250 rentals |
| Tote attrition | 3–5% / year | Loss, theft, damage; branded totes reduce theft |
| Variable cost / move | $25–28 | Delivery labor $12–15, pickup $3–5, fuel $6–8, amortization ~$2.75 |
| Pickup model | Centralized | Tenant drops at designated spot; one stop per complex, batched |
| Rental period | 14 days | Standard window; rush delivery available at +$35/move |

## Tote counts per unit type

Based on Totes on Loan (totesonloan.com) operational data, Fort Wayne IN:

| Unit type | Totes per move | Price per move | Contribution margin |
|---|---|---|---|
| Studio | 20 | $100 | $72–75 |
| 1BR | 30 | $120 | $92–95 |
| 2BR | 40 | $150 | $122–125 |
| 3BR+ | 55+ | $190 | $162–165 |
| **Blended avg** | **~35** | **~$130** | **~$102–105** |

Unit mix assumption (Denver metro apartment stock): Studio 25%, 1BR 30%, 2BR 30%, 3BR+ 15%.

## Revenue streams

| Stream | Revenue | Cost to us | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Per-move fee | $100–190 / move | $25–28 variable | Billed monthly to complex commercial account |
| Setup fee | 50% of tote cost | $0 (covers inventory) | One-time; covers onboarding + branding |
| Rush delivery | +$35 / move | ~$10 incremental | Same-day / weekend surcharge |
| Storage referral | $50–75 / referral | $0 | Commission from storage company; ~20% of moves |
| Moving co. referral | $30–50 / referral | $0 | Commission from mover; ~25% of moves |

Blended referral contribution: ~$19/move at zero incremental cost. Effective revenue per move with referrals: ~$149 blended.

# Package structure

Packages are pegged to tote inventory, not move counts. The complex manages allocation across their incoming tenants.

| Package | Totes | Setup fee | Your capital (50%) | Fits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | 150 | $1,500 | $1,500 | Small complex, ~200 units |
| Growth | 300 | $3,000 | $3,000 | Mid complex, ~350 units |
| Portfolio | 600 | $6,000 | $6,000 | Large complex or 2–3 smaller |
| Enterprise | Custom | Custom | Custom | Regional PMC, multi-property |

Setup fee = 50% of tote cost at $20/tote. You own the totes. Setup fee covers onboarding, site walkthrough, and custom branding.

## Concurrent move capacity

Each tenant holds totes for ~14 days. Concurrent capacity = total totes / blended totes per move (~35).

| Package | Totes | Max concurrent moves | Approx. moves / month |
|---|---|---|---|
| Starter | 150 | ~4 | ~8 |
| Growth | 300 | ~8 | ~15 |
| Portfolio | 600 | ~15 | ~30 |

Moves/month assumes 2x turnover of concurrent capacity per 14-day cycle. Actual throughput depends on complex scheduling and return compliance.

# Growth model by scale

| Scale | Monthly fixed | Annual fixed | Includes |

| 1–3 complexes | $225 | $2,700 | Storage $130, insurance $75, misc $20 |

# Breakeven analysis

## Breakeven by complex count at each cost tier

| Fixed cost tier | Annual fixed | Breakeven moves / year |
|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 (1–3 complexes) | $2,700 | 22 moves ($2,700 / $124 contribution) |
| Tier 2 (4–10 complexes) | $6,000 | 48 moves ($6,000 / $124 contribution) |
| Tier 3 (11–20 complexes) | $37,200 | 300 moves ($37,200 / $124 contribution) |

Contribution margin per move: $149 revenue – $25 avg variable = $124. At 80 moves/complex/year, Tier 1 breaks even in the first month of the first complex. Tier 2 breaks even at ~1 complex. Tier 3 breaks even at ~4 complexes (but you have 11+ at that point, so you clear it immediately).

## Breakeven by number of facilities

The question: at each fixed cost tier, how many active complexes do you need to cover all fixed costs?

| Fixed cost tier | Annual fixed | Net contribution / complex | Complexes to breakeven |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 | $2,700 | $9,920 | **1 (covers 3.7x)** |
| Tier 2 | $6,000 | $9,920 | **1 (covers 1.7x)** |
| Tier 3 | $37,200 | $10,520 | **4 (of 11+ operating)** |

Net contribution per complex = (80 moves x $149 revenue) – (80 moves x $25 variable) = $9,920/year at Tier 1–2 rates, $10,520/year at Tier 3 rates (lower variable cost). The business is breakeven-positive from the first complex at every tier.

# Key growth milestones

| Milestone | What happens | Net profit | Decision |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 complex | Proof of concept; validate logistics | $7K/yr | Confirm PM adoption, loss rates, pickup model |
| 3 complexes | Real side business | $26K/yr | Quit iterating, start systematizing |
| 5 complexes | Serious income | $44K/yr | Consider dedicated vehicle |
| 10 complexes | Inflection point | $93K/yr | Max before hiring; evaluate PT driver |
| 11 complexes | Hire PT driver | $85K/yr | Margin compresses; growth recovers it |
| 15 complexes | Real small business | $115K/yr | Acquisition / franchise-ready |
| 20 complexes | Scale business | $166K/yr | Regional expansion or second market |

The 10–11 complex transition is the critical inflection. At 10 complexes you net $93K running every route yourself. At 11 you hire a PT driver ($23.4K/year) and net drops to ~$85K — but your time is freed and the path to 15–20 complexes opens up. The margin recovers by complex 13–14.

# Market context

**Denver metro apartment units:** 434,465 across ~1,960 communities (AAMD Q4 2024)

**Annual turnover:** ~40%, producing ~173,800 moves/year

**B2B-addressable segment (50+ unit complexes):** ~104,000 moves/year

**TAM at $130 blended/move:** ~$13.5M/year Denver metro

**Current vacancy:** 7.6% (16-year high); record concessions at ~$676/unit/year

**Competitive position:** Stack (stackmoves.com) operates B2C only; B2B apartment channel is unoccupied (confirmed 2026-03-26)

**Timing window:** 12–24 months before supply-demand rebalances and concession pressure eases

# Key risks and sensitivities

| Risk | Severity | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stack pivots to B2B | High | Direct competition from funded national player | Speed + long contracts; build PMC relationships first |
| Tote loss/theft | Medium | Replacement cost erodes margin | Branded totes harder to repurpose; PM pickup accountability; deposit option |
| PM contract churn | Medium | Revenue loss; stranded branded inventory | Target PMC portfolio relationships, not individual PMs; long contracts |
| Tote cost increase | Low | Raises capital requirement and setup fees | Lock bulk pricing early; 10% cost increase = $2/tote, manageable |
| Variable cost creep | Low | Fuel, labor inflation | Route optimization; geographic clustering of complexes |
| Seasonal cash flow | Low | Peak May–Aug, slow Nov–Feb | Plan Q4 cash cushion; use slow months for sales pipeline |

## Sensitivity: price per move

Every $10 increase in blended price adds $800/year per complex at zero additional cost. At 10 complexes, a $10 price increase = $8,000 additional annual profit.

## Sensitivity: tote cost

If bulk tote cost rises from $20 to $25 (+25%), setup fees increase proportionally ($1,875 Starter vs. $1,500). Your capital per complex increases by $250 for Starter. Impact on per-move economics is negligible ($0.02/rental amortization increase).

## Sensitivity: move volume per complex

Model assumes 80 moves/complex/year (200-unit building, 40% turnover). A 300-unit building produces ~120 moves/year, increasing per-complex contribution by 50%. A 150-unit building produces ~60 moves/year, reducing contribution by 25%. Target larger complexes first.
